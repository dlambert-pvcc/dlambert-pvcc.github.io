Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling
Define these terms:
1: scientific method
Observe, hypothesize, experiment to prove or disprove. Record the results.
2: computational model
Modeling a system through relevant math and algorithms rather than as a physical substitute.
3: discrete event simulation
Applying time within a model only as or when it's relevant.

List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation model:
1. Existence: The real thing might not yet exist.
2. Physical realization: The real thing may be incorporeal.
3. Safety: The real thing may not be safe to experiment on at scale.
4. Speed of construction: It might take too long to build the real thing.
5. Time scale: The real thing may work too fast or too slow to experiment on.
6. Ethical behavior: It might be really messed up to work on the real thing.
7. Ease of modification: It might be a huge pain in the butt to adjust the real thing.
