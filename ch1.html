Three misconceptions about Computer Science:
1. Computer science is the study of computers.
2. Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs.
3. Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.

Briefly define:
1. Computer Science
The study of algorithms.
2. algorithm
An ordered sequence of unambiguous instructions that produce a result before halting.
3. sequential operation
Do x, then y, then z.
4. conditional operation
Do x if y.
5. iterative operation
Do x until y.
6. stored program computer
A computer that stores programmed instructions internally.
7. programmer
Someone who writes commands in a high-level language (C++, Python, etc., instead of binary).
8. integrated circuit
Transistors, resistors, and capacitors photographically etched onto a piece of silicon.

Briefly describe these computer generations:
1. First generation computer
Used vacuum tubes; difficult and expensive to maintain. Operated only by trained personnel in laboratories.
2. Second generation computer
Used transistors, which reduced cost and increased reliability. First used high-level programming languages.
3. Third generation computer
Used integrated circuits; desk-sized. Beneficiary of the birth of the software industry for specialized programs.
4. Fourth generation computer
Desktop-sized. (Some of them) used novice-friendly graphical user interfaces. Capable of networking with other computers.
5. Fifth generation computer
Further reduction in size and cost. May include parallel processors, high-resolution 3D graphics, biometric scans, or cloud storage. Nearly every modern appliance, no matter how large or small, contains one.

FORTRAN:
1. What does the acronym FORTRAN stand for?
Formula translation.
2. When was FORTRAN developed?
Development started in late 1953; it was released commercially in 1957.
3. What was it used for?
Complex economic and statistical models.
4. Is FORTRAN still being used today? If so, what for?
It is. IBM's website says it's still used "in Doppler radar weather forecasts and atmospheric and oceanic studies, as well as in simulating nanoparticles, genomes, DNA and atomic structures."
Sources: https://www.ibm.com/history/fortran

COBOL:
1. What does the acronym COBOL stand for?
Common business-oriented language.
2. When was COBOL developed?
Development started in 1959; it was released in 1960.
3. What was it used for?
"Large-precision fixed-point decimal calculations", as noted on IBM's website. Well suited for financial and administrative systems.
4. Is COBOL still being used today? If so, what for?
It is. It's still a popular language for banking systems.
Sources: https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/cobol
