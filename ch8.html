Briefly describe or define:
1. information security
"[P]rotecting data from those who do not have permission to access it."
2. authentication
Proving you are who you're supposed to be when accessing a machine or service.
3. authorization
Whether or not you, being who you're supposed to be, have permission to access a machine or service.
4. dual authentication
Making an extra code necessary to log in after entering your password. The extra code is generated for only a few seconds.
5. social engineering
"Hello, [first name, from LinkedIn], this is Nate from [security firm your company contracts, from the clients listed on their website]. [Your company] is doing some security training this week and you are next on my list of who to complete that with. Are you at your computer right now?"
6. virus
Malware activated upon opening or running an infected file.
7. worm
Malware that can spread through nodes on its own.
8. Trojan horse
A mostly legitimate program, but carries malware underneath its otherwise legit purpose.
9. ransomware
Malware that locks parts of or your entire computer. Want access back? Here's where you can wire a payment.
10. phishing
Not malware, but social engineering on a wider and usually impersonal scale. Emails with links to websites that can steal sensitive data.
11. White Hat
Someone who pursues bug bounties or cracks systems in order to inform administrators of vulnerabilities that need patching. Usually legal.
12. Black Hat
Someone who cracks systems for profit, "lulz," or to find private information. Usually illegal.
13. encryption/decryption
Encryption: using algorithms to scramble messsages such that only people you want to be able to read them can do so. Decryption: doing so. What Alan Turing and his team did to help beat the Nazis their first go around.
14. cryptography
"[T]he science of 'secret writing.'" Encryption and decryption as a professional or academic field.
15. symmetric encryption algorithm
Encryption that requires both sender and receiver have the same secret key.
16. Caesar cipher
A kind of cipher where each letter is replaced with another letter at a fixed distance. Classic choice for Nancy Drew mystery games.
17. block cipher
A kind of cipher where each letter or character gets encrypted into multiple other scattered characters.
18. Data Encryption Standard (DES)
Developed by IBM and certified by the International Organization for Standardization to protect electronic information.
19. public key system
An assymetric encryption algorithm where the encryption key is publically known, but the decryption key is specific to the receiver of a message.
20. embedded computers
Computers or computer chips within something you don't traditionally think of as a computer, like thermostats, cameras, or cars.

List six ways to protect yourself from most cyber attacks
1. Up-to-date antivirus software.
2. Up-to-date firewall software.
3. Up-to-date antispyware.
4. Pop-up blocker(s).
5. Your OS's latest security patches or updates.
6. Don't reply to scam emails.
